# Dataset Notes

This file serves as the dataset documentation for the IPKE public dataset slice (issue 53).
It follows the datasheet format of Gebru et al. (2021) "Datasheets for Datasets" (CACM).
It is the primary source for the dataset section of the paper.

---

## Motivation

**Why was this dataset created?**
To evaluate procedural knowledge extraction from industrial safety and laboratory
procedure documents. Existing benchmarks (ProPara, OpenPI) focus on entity state changes
or open-domain procedures. None target the constraint-attachment problem: identifying
safety constraints and linking them to the specific procedural step they govern. This
dataset fills that gap for safety-critical industrial document domains.

**Who created it?**
Primary annotator: Imad Eddine Elmouss (author 1). AI-assisted drafting via frontier
model (GPT-4o), followed by human correction. Human correction status is tracked per
file in the quality block of each gold JSON.

documents requiring paid access.

## Inter-Annotator Agreement

### Methodology

Double annotation was performed for 2 of 8 Tier-A documents (25% of the set).
The paper target is IAA on ≥ 30% of the final dataset. This slice is below that
target; additional second-pass annotations are required before submission.

Kappa was computed using token-label agreement (compute_iaa.py, `token_label_kappa`).
The scale used for interpretation is Landis and Koch (1977):

| κ range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| < 0.20 | Slight |
| 0.21-0.40 | Fair |
| 0.41-0.60 | Moderate |
| 0.61-0.80 | Substantial |
| 0.81-1.00 | Almost perfect |

The minimum acceptable for a paper IAA claim is substantial (κ ≥ 0.61).

### Per-document results

| doc_id | annotator_A | annotator_B | token_label_kappa | Landis-Koch | paper status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| epa_field_sampling_measurement_procedure_validation | author 1 | author 2 | 0.929 | Almost perfect | PASS |
| olsk_small_cnc_v1_workbook | author 1 | author 2 | 0.531 | Moderate | **FLAG: needs re-annotation** |

### Aggregate (across 2 docs)

- step_exact F1: 0.778
- constraint_exact F1: 0.889
- relation_exact F1: 0.889
- token_label_kappa: 0.709 (across 219 token-label pairs)
- Landis-Koch: Substantial

The aggregate kappa (0.709) is in the "substantial" range, but is pulled up by the EPA
document. **The OLSK CNC document (κ = 0.531, moderate) must not be included in any
IAA claim until re-annotated.** Report IAA on the EPA document only until OLSK is
re-done: κ = 0.929 (almost perfect, 1 document).

### Adjudication protocol

Disagreements are resolved by the primary annotator (author 1) reviewing both
annotations and selecting the reading better supported by the source text. The
adjudicated version is committed to `datasets/paper/gold/`. The second-annotator
version is archived in `datasets/paper/second_pass/`.

**Disclosure required in paper:** number of documents double-annotated, kappa per
dimension and aggregate, adjudication method. See Pustejovsky and Stubbs (2012)
"Natural Language Annotation for Machine Learning" §3 for the standard disclosure.

## Constraint metric threshold

The `ConstraintAttachmentF1` metric uses embedding cosine similarity (sentence-transformers
`all-mpnet-base-v2`) to match predicted constraint text to gold constraint text, then
checks whether the attached step also aligns. A threshold governs what cosine score
counts as a match.

**Empirical check (2026-06-06):** The metric was run at thresholds 0.70, 0.75, 0.80,
and 0.85 on the two thesis gold documents with available predictions
(`DOA_Food_Man_Proc_Stor`, `op_firesafety_guideline`). Both thresholds 0.75 and 0.85
produced `ConstraintCoverage = 0.0` and `ConstraintAttachmentF1 = 0.0` — the sample
(8 gold constraints total) is too small to distinguish threshold sensitivity. 0.85 also
reduced StepF1 (stricter step alignment hurts recall). There is no empirical basis to
raise the threshold from 0.75.

**Default is 0.75.** The paper must disclose: "Constraint attachment was evaluated
using semantic fuzzy matching (all-mpnet-base-v2, cosine threshold 0.75) because
strict string equality produced near-zero scores due to paraphrase variation in LLM
output. The threshold was held constant across all configurations."

## Limitations

- n=8 is a small evaluation set. All claims have wide confidence intervals. Multi-seed
  runs (N=5) and 95% CI are required to be reported next to every number.
- Single industrial-document focus. Does not cover conversational procedures, recipes,
  or customer-facing instructions.
- English only.
- Gold annotations are AI-drafted; human correction is incomplete.
- IAA covers 25% of the dataset; 2/2 documents from a single domain family (EPA/OLSK).
  Not representative of the full domain range.
- NASA and EPA directives can be policy-heavy; step-level extraction quality may not
  generalize to operational SOPs.
- Raw PDFs are not committed. Reproduction depends on stable source URLs and manifest
  SHA-256 hashes.

## Source families and selection criteria

The following criteria were applied to include a document:

1. Publicly accessible without paywalls.
2. Contains ordered procedural steps with at least one conditional clause (if/when/unless).
3. 300-2000 words per excerpt.
4. At least 3 distinct domain families in the set.

Documents excluded: iFixit and MyFixit (licensing risk), paywall-gated ASTM/ISO
standards, documents without branching logic.
